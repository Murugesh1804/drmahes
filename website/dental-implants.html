What Are Dental Implants?

The Gold Standard for Missing Teeth

A dental implant is a small titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone to act as an artificial tooth root. Once it fuses with the bone (a process called osseointegration), a natural-looking ceramic crown is attached — giving you a tooth that is indistinguishable from your own.

Unlike dentures or bridges, implants preserve jawbone, prevent facial sagging and require no special maintenance beyond regular brushing and flossing. With proper care, they can last a lifetime.

Ideal for: Single missing tooth, multiple missing teeth, full arch restoration (All-on-4), or to support dentures. Suitable for most adults with adequate bone density.
Why Choose Implants

Benefits of Dental Implants

Natural Appearance
Custom-crafted ceramic crowns match the colour, shape and translucency of your natural teeth — nobody will know the difference.
Full Bite Strength
Implants restore up to 90% of your natural bite force — eat anything you like, from hard foods to sticky sweets, with confidence.
Preserves Jawbone
The titanium post stimulates the jawbone just like a natural root, preventing the bone loss that occurs with dentures or gaps.
Lifetime Durability
With proper oral hygiene and regular check-ups, implants are designed to last 20–25 years or more — a true lifetime investment.
Easy Maintenance
No soaking, adhesives or special cleaners. Clean implants exactly like natural teeth — brush, floss and rinse.
Confidence Restored
Implants eliminate the self-consciousness of gaps or removable dentures, giving you freedom to speak, laugh and smile fully.
The Process

What to Expect

The implant journey is carefully planned across multiple visits to ensure the best possible outcome.

1
Consultation & Digital X-Ray
Dr. Maheswari evaluates your bone density, gum health and overall oral condition using digital X-rays to determine suitability and create a precise treatment plan.
2
Implant Placement (Under Local Anaesthesia)
The titanium post is placed into the jawbone through a minor surgical procedure. The area is numbed completely — most patients report minimal discomfort.
3
Healing & Osseointegration (8–12 Weeks)
The implant fuses with the jawbone over 8–12 weeks. During this time, a temporary restoration may be placed so you always have a complete smile.
4
Crown Placement
Once fully healed, a custom-fabricated ceramic crown is attached to the implant. The crown is colour-matched and shaped to blend seamlessly with your other teeth.
Questions & Answers

Implant FAQs

The procedure is done under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during the surgery. Mild soreness and swelling for 2–3 days after is normal and easily managed with prescribed medication. Most patients are surprised by how comfortable the process is.

With proper care, implants can last 20–25 years or even a lifetime. The crown on top may need replacement after 10–15 years due to normal wear, but the implant post itself is designed to be permanent.

Most healthy adults with adequate jawbone density are good candidates. Conditions such as uncontrolled diabetes or active gum disease need to be treated first. Dr. Maheswari will assess your suitability during the initial consultation.

From the first consultation to the final crown, the process typically takes 3–6 months. Most of this time is the natural healing phase. The actual surgical appointments are relatively quick and straightforward.
