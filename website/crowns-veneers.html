Crowns vs Veneers

Restore Strength, Restore Beauty

Dental Crowns are tooth-shaped caps placed over the entire visible portion of a damaged tooth. They restore full strength, shape, and appearance — used after root canals, for broken teeth, or as the visible component of a dental implant.

Veneers are thin shells of porcelain or composite bonded to only the front surface of teeth — used purely for aesthetic improvements like changing colour, shape or hiding cracks, without altering the entire tooth.

Crown materials we use: Zirconia (strongest, most lifelike), e.max lithium disilicate (excellent aesthetics), and PFM — Porcelain Fused to Metal (traditional, durable). Dr. Maheswari will recommend the best material for your tooth's location and function.
Why Choose Us

Benefits of Our Crowns & Veneers

Superior Strength
Zirconia crowns are exceptionally strong — able to withstand the same biting forces as natural tooth structure, ideal for back teeth and heavy biters.
Natural Aesthetics
Our crown materials transmit light similarly to natural enamel, giving a lifelike translucency that blends seamlessly with surrounding teeth.
Protects Damaged Teeth
Crowns prevent cracked or weakened teeth from fracturing further. After root canal treatment, a crown is essential to protect the tooth long-term.
Implant-Compatible
Our zirconia and e.max crowns are the gold standard for topping dental implants, providing the most natural-looking and durable final restoration.
Long-Lasting
With proper care, zirconia crowns can last 15–20 years or more. Veneers typically last 10–15 years. Regular check-ups maximise their lifespan.
Minimal Tooth Removal (Veneers)
Modern veneer preparation removes only a tiny layer of enamel — far less invasive than crowns, making veneers an ideal choice for cosmetic corrections.
The Process

Getting a Crown — Step by Step

1
Consultation & Shade Matching
Dr. Maheswari examines the tooth, assesses which material is most appropriate, and uses a shade guide to select the colour that best matches your natural teeth.
2
Tooth Preparation
The tooth is gently shaped under local anaesthesia to create space for the crown. An impression or digital scan of the prepared tooth is taken and sent to the dental laboratory.
3
Temporary Crown
A temporary crown is placed to protect the prepared tooth while the permanent crown is being fabricated in the lab (typically 5–7 working days).
4
Final Crown Fitting
The permanent crown is tried in, checked for fit, bite and colour. Once you are happy, it is permanently cemented. Your tooth is now fully restored and protected.
FAQs

Crown & Veneer Questions

The process typically takes 2 visits spread over 1–2 weeks. The first visit involves tooth preparation and impressions; the second visit (after the crown is fabricated in the lab) involves permanent fitting. Some clinics offer same-day CEREC crowns, but the conventional 2-visit process allows more detailed customisation.

Zirconia is recommended for its superior strength and great aesthetics — ideal for both front and back teeth. E.max offers exceptional translucency and is often preferred for front teeth where aesthetics are paramount. PFM is a traditional, reliable option but can show a dark metal line at the gum margin over time. Dr. Maheswari will recommend the right material based on the tooth's location and your specific case.

Tooth preparation is done under local anaesthesia, so you will not feel pain during the procedure. Some mild sensitivity to temperature is common for a few days while wearing the temporary crown, which settles once the permanent crown is placed.

Crowns are permanently cemented and are designed not to come off. However, in rare cases, the cement can break down over many years. If your crown feels loose, avoid hard foods and contact the clinic immediately — re-cementation is a straightforward procedure.
