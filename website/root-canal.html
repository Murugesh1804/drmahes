What Is Root Canal Treatment?

Painless Relief, Natural Tooth Saved

A root canal (RCT) is needed when the soft pulp inside your tooth becomes infected or inflamed due to deep decay, a cracked tooth, or repeated dental procedures. Left untreated, this infection spreads and can lead to abscess or tooth loss.

During the procedure, Dr. Maheswari removes the infected pulp, thoroughly cleans and shapes the root canals, fills them with a biocompatible material, and seals the tooth. Modern techniques and anaesthesia make this no more uncomfortable than a routine filling.

Signs you may need RCT: Severe toothache, sensitivity to hot/cold that lingers, darkening of the tooth, swelling or tenderness in nearby gums, or a persistent pimple on the gums.
Why Choose RCT

Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

Immediate Pain Relief

Root canal treatment directly addresses the source of tooth pain. Most patients feel significant relief within 24–48 hours of the procedure.

Saves Your Natural Tooth

Keeping your natural tooth maintains jawbone integrity and avoids the need for more expensive replacement options like implants or bridges.

Stops Infection Spreading

Left untreated, dental infections spread to neighbouring teeth, jaw and potentially the bloodstream. RCT eliminates the infection at its source.

Single-Sitting Option

In many cases, Dr. Maheswari can complete the entire root canal procedure in a single visit — minimising your time in the chair and recovery period.

Long-Lasting Results

With a crown placed afterward, a root-canal-treated tooth can function comfortably for 10–15 years or more with proper care.

Restore Normal Function

Eat, speak and bite normally again. The treated tooth feels and functions like any other tooth once the crown is placed.
The Procedure

How Root Canal Treatment Works

The procedure is carefully performed under local anaesthesia — most patients are surprised by how comfortable it is.

1

Diagnosis & X-Ray

Digital X-rays pinpoint the extent of the infection and help Dr. Maheswari plan the procedure precisely, including the length and shape of each root canal.
2

Local Anaesthesia & Isolation

The tooth is numbed completely. A rubber dam is placed to keep the area clean, dry and protected throughout the procedure.
3

Pulp Removal & Canal Cleaning

The infected pulp is removed and the root canals are carefully shaped and disinfected with irrigating solutions to eliminate all bacteria.
4

Filling & Sealing

The cleaned canals are filled with gutta-percha (a biocompatible rubber material) and sealed. A temporary or permanent filling is then placed.
5

Crown Placement

A custom-made ceramic crown is placed over the treated tooth to protect it from future fractures and restore its full strength and appearance.
